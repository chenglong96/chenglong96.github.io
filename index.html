New Player Retention in Torn

Abstract

Purpose: New player retention and engagement are crucial elements for any online video game. Without a stable number of newly registered accounts, multiplayer games centered around community and player interaction are bound to become abandoned and fail. In this study, I aim to evaluate the new player retention rate of an online role-playing game called Torn.

Study design: Using publicly available information, I have analysed 367,078 player accounts created between July 1 2023 and July 1 2024.

Results: The first few days of gameplay are crucial for player retention. Over the first couple of months, player retention rate steadily decreases, eventually settling down around 18–20% after the first year. Level seems to be another important factor when it comes to players staying in the game — majority of players quitting do so before reaching level 15, an important milestone in the Torn gameplay loop.

Conclusions: My findings highlight potential shortfalls of the game design and provide some basic guidelines when considering potential recruits for new members when forming in-game groups (factions).
